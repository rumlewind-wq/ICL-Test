# Domicile, art 4 and 62
Article 62

1.   In order to determine whether a party is domiciled in the Member State whose courts are seised of a matter, ==the court shall apply its internal law==.

2.   If a party is not domiciled in the Member State whose courts are seised of the matter, then, in order to determine whether the party is domiciled in another Member State, the court shall apply the law of that Member State.

- National concepts
- Some variety
- Several domiciles
- No EU domicile

---

**==(CHAT:==**
**Article 62 (Natural persons):**

- Domicile determined by **internal law** of the seised MS.
- If domiciled in another MS → Apply that MS’ law.
- Result: National definitions, variation between MS, possible multiple domiciles.


# Seat/domicile, Brussel I, art 4 and 63
Article 63

For the purposes of this Regulation, a company or other legal person or association of natural or legal persons is domiciled at the place where it has its:

  (a)  statutory seat; 

  (b)  central administration; or 

  (c)  principal place of business. 
  
- Uniform concept
- Internal organization has external consequences
- Shareholders’ choice
- Limited forum shopping

---
**==CHAT:==**
**Article 63 (Legal persons):**

- Uniform EU rule: Domicile where:
    - (a) statutory seat,
    - (b) central administration, or
    - (c) principal place of business.
- Prevents excessive forum shopping but still allows some choice by companies.)

# Contract jurisdiction

Contract jurisdiction, Brussels I, art. 7.1

A person domiciled in a Member State may be sued in another Member State:

(1)(a) in matters relating to a contract, in the courts for the place of performance of the obligation in question

(b) for the purpose of this provision and unless otherwise agreed, the place of performance of the obligation in question shall be:
- in the case of the sale of goods, the place in a Member State where, under the contract, the goods were delivered or should have been delivered,
- in the case of the provision of services, the place in a Member State where, under the contract, the services were provided or should have been provided;

(c) if point (b) does not apply then point (a) applies.


- (a) General rule: Place of performance of obligation.
- (b) Specific rules:
    - Sale of goods → Delivery place in MS.
    - Services → Place where services provided.
- (c) If neither applies → fallback to (a).

**==CHAT:==**
- Key case: _Bier, Case 21/76_ → “place where the harmful event occurred” includes:
    - Place of **event giving rise to harm**.
    - Place where **damage occurred**.
- _Shevill, C-68/93_: Defamation → plaintiff may sue:
    - Entire harm → defendant’s domicile.
    - Local harm → courts in each affected MS.
- _eDate, C-509/09_ and _Martinez, C-161/10_: Online defamation → plaintiff may sue at:
    - Center of interests for full damage,
    - Or each MS for local damage.
- _Tacconi, C-334/00_: Pre-contractual liability → tort jurisdiction.

	- Natural: determined by internal law of the seised MS, if domiciled in another MS - that MS' law apply.  (Art. 62)

4. How do the special rules for sales of goods and provisions of services work?
	- unless agreed otherwise, the place of performance of the obligation in question shall be where the goods or services, under the contract, is delivered or provided.

	- the place of tort, delict or quasi-delict is where the obligation shall be done
