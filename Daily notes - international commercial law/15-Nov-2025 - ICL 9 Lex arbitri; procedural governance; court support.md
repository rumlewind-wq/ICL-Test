### Applicable laws and rules- Issues

- The law governing the dispute/the contract
	- A national law
	- Lex mercatoria
	- Party autonomy

- The law governing the arbitral proceedings
	- The lex arbitri
	- Party autonomy

- The governing law- other issues

### The law governing the dispute/the contract

- The law governing the container contract
	- Can be a national law, related or unrelated to one of the parties, the CISG, or the lex mercatoria, or even ex aequo et bono

- The choice of law rules are determined by the lex arbitra

- Model Law art. 28(1) and(4)
	- Parties may choose the"The rules of law"
		- National law, the CISG, or the lex mercatoria
	- Parties may also choose ex aequo et bono

### The law governing the dispute/the contract

The law governing the container contract when the parties have not chosen it:

- Model Law art. 28(1)
	- the Arbitral Tribunal shall apply the law determined by the choice of law rules the Tribunal considers applicable(voie indirecte)

- ICC Rules, art. 22(1)
	- the Arbitral Tribunal shall apply the rules of law which it determines to be appropriate(voie directe)

- 2010 Uncitral Rules
	- the Arbitral Tribunal shall apply the law which it determines to be appropriate(voie directe)

### The law governing the dispute/the contract

- The lex mercatoria- the law governing the"container"contract
	- The governing law is usually a national law of a State
	- Not necessarily well-suited for international contracts
	- The invention of the lex mercatoria

### The law governing the dispute/the contract

- The lex mercatoria, characteristics
	- A loosely organized system of transnational legal principles derived from usages, customs and practice of international commerce
	- General principles of law and transnational rules
	- Customary commercial law

### The law governing the dispute/the contract

The lex mercatoria, characteristics, cont.
- A set of substantive rules for contracts, such as
	- Unidroit Principles of International Commercial Contracts
	- Principles of European Contract Law
	- CISG
	- ICC Incoterms

### The law governing the dispute/the dispute

- Application of lex mercatoria

- Contracts between two states
	- Lex mercatoria is State neutral

- Contracts between a state and a private company
	- Risk of undermining the law by new legislation
	- Freezing clauses

### The lex arbitrari Governs the arbitral proceedings

- Lex arbitrai is the law governing the arbitral proceedings

- A national law/statute on arbitration
	- e.g. the English Arbitration Act or the Danish Arbitration Act

### The lex arbitra Governs the arbitral proceedings

- The rules governing the arbitral proceedings
	- The lex arbitri usually has only a few mandatory provisions
	- Much room for procedural party autonomy
	- Ad hoc arbitration
	- Institutional arbitration

### The lex arbitra Governs the arbitral proceedings

- Lex arbitrari covers in particular these issues:
	- Arbitrability and public policy
	- The arbitration agreement
	- Composition and establishment of the tribunal and impartiality and independence
	- Conduct of arbitral proceedings
	- Making of the award and formal requirements
	- Invalidity of the award
	- Recognition and enforcement of the award

### The lex arbitrari Governs the arbitral proceedings

- The parties are free to choose the lex arbitra
	- The Model law art. 20
	- Most institutional rules

- Failing an agreement on the lex arbitra
	- The arbitral tribunal determines its own seat, Model Law art. 20
	- In institutional arbitration, the institution or the Tribunal will determine the seat

### Applicable laws and rules- other issues

The law governing the arbitration agreement
- The lex arbitrati usually governs formal validity of the arbitration agreement
- Which law governs the substantive validity of it?
- Relevant question when one party resists arbitration,when the award is challenged, or when enforcement is resisted
- The governing law is decided under the lex arbitri's provisions on this question

### Applicable laws and rules- other issues

- Different solutions
	- ICC award Case No. 6162, the seat law governs validity
	- ICC award Case No. 2626, the law governing the container contract governs validity of the clause
	- Swiss PIL Act, art. 178(2), the clause is valid if valid under either the law chosen by the parties, the law governing the container contract or Swiss law
	- The Model Law, art. 34(2), the clause is valid, if valid under the law chosen by the parties to govern the clause or, if no such law was chosen, the seat law

### Applicable laws and rules- other issues

- The law governing arbitrability
	- The seat law, the law chosen by the parties, the law governing the container contract or the law at the place of enforcement?

- The Model Law, art. 34(2)(b)(i)
	- The seat law governs arbitrability during the proceedings

- The NYC, art. V(2)(a) and Model Law art. 36(1)(b)(i)
	- The law of enforcement governs arbitrability at the enforce-ment stage

### Applicable laws and rules- other issues

- The law governing public policy
	- The seat law, the law chosen by the parties, the law governing the container contract or the law at the place of enforcement?

- The Model Law, art. 34(2)(b)(ii)
	- The seat law governs public policy during the proceedings

- The NYC, art. V(2)(b) and Model Law art. 36(1)(b)(ii)
	- The law of enforcement governs arbitrability at the enforce-ment stage

### Applicable laws and rules- other issues

An arbitrator's duty to apply the law
- Today's expectations, apply the law, unless the parties have chosen ex aequo et bono
- Duty to render an enforceable award, according to several institutional rules and be aware of the New York Convention
- Duty to follow the lex arbitra
- Duty to apply mandatory provisions of the lex arbitri

2) Can they agree on the law governing the container contract?

### Case 2, lecture 9

A from Germany and B from Norway are negotiating a contract with an arbitration agreement. They agreed that the seat of the arbitral tribunal should be in Denmark.

1) Which law governs the formal validity of the arbitration agreement?

2) Which law governs the substantive validity of the arbitration agreement?

3) Which law governs arbitrability, when the proceedings are initiated?

4) And if the award is to be enforced in Germany?
