# Choice of court agreements, art. 25
1.   If the ==parties==, regardless of their domicile, ==have agreed== that a court or the courts of a Member State are to have jurisdiction to settle any disputes which have arisen or which may arise in connection with a particular legal relationship, that court or those courts shall have jurisdiction, ==unless the agreement is null== and void as to its substantive validity under the law of that Member State. Such jurisdiction shall be exclusive unless the parties have agreed otherwise. The agreement conferring jurisdiction shall be either:
- (a) in writing or evidenced in writing; 
- (b) in a form which accords with practices which the parties have established between themselves; or 
- (c) in international trade or commerce, in a form which accords with a usage of
	  which the parties are or ought to have been aware and which in such  trade or commerce is widely known to, and regularly observed by,   parties to contracts of the type involved in the particular trade or   commerce concerned.

… 

5.   An agreement conferring jurisdiction which forms part of a contract shall be treated as an agreement independent of the other terms of the contract. The validity of the agreement conferring jurisdiction cannot be contested solely on the ground that the contract is not valid.1.

# [[Lis pendens]], art. 29
Article 29

1.   Without prejudice to Article 31(2), where proceedings involving the same cause of action and between the same parties are brought in the courts of different Member States, any court other than the court first seised shall of its own motion stay its proceedings until such time as the jurisdiction of the court first seised is established.

2.   In cases referred to in paragraph 1, upon request by a court seised of the dispute, any other court seised shall without delay inform the former court of the date when it was seised in accordance with Article 32.

3.   Where the jurisdiction of the court first seised is established, any court other than the court first seised shall decline jurisdiction in favour of that court.

# lis pendens and choice of court agreements, art. 31.2

1.   Where actions come within the exclusive jurisdiction of several courts, any court other than the court first seised shall decline jurisdiction in favour of that court.

2.   Without prejudice to Article 26, where a court of a Member State on which an agreement as referred to in Article 25 confers exclusive jurisdiction is seised, any court of another Member State shall stay the proceedings until such time as the court seised on the basis of the agreement declares that it has no jurisdiction under the agreement.

3.   Where the court designated in the agreement has established jurisdiction in accordance with the agreement, any court of another Member State shall decline jurisdiction in favour of that court.

4.   Paragraphs 2 and 3 shall not apply to matters referred to in Sections 3, 4 or 5 where the policyholder, the insured, a beneficiary of the insurance contract, the injured party, the consumer or the employee is the claimant and the agreement is not valid under a provision contained within those Sections.



# lis pendens and choice of court agreements, art. 31.2
- New provision
	- A reverse lis pendens mechanism
	- Only exclusive choice of court agreements
	- Only triggered if proceedings instituted before the agreed court
	- Only applicable if chosen court has jurisdiction under the agreement, not, for instance, Art 7.1.
- The mechanism applied to Gasser


# Questions, Lecture 3
1. How are consumers protected under Brussels I?
	- consumers can only be sued in their home member state as they are the weaker party.
	- consumers are intitled to sue the company in the consumers domicile, art. 18.1
		- eller i det land, hvor virksomheden hører til.
		- businesses cannot force consumers into a foreign forum through unilateral agreements. 


2. Is it all types of consumer contracts that are protective in nature?
	- no, instalment credit term sales, loan repayable by installment or other credit form is protected in another way, art. 17. 
	- private, non-commercial agreements are excluded



3. What are the effects of an exclusive choice of court agreement?
	- Under art. 25, a valid exclusive jurisdiction clause gives the chosen court mandatory jurisdiction, overriding general rules.
	- all other courts must decline jurisdiction, even if they were seized first.
	- the agreement must meet [[Formal validity]] requirements (written, electronic confirmation, or established trade practice)
	- execption: insumer, insurance and employment contexts, protection rules limit enforceability unless the clause was agreed after the dispuse arose or specifically allowed under brussels 1. 
	- 
	- Mit svar:
	- It bypasses the lis pendens povision in art. 29, and uses the reverse lis pendens provision in 31.  
		- the parties cant be italian torpedoed, and the agreed upon choice of court can proceed with the problem even if it was not the first court to be seised by the party.  


4. What is the purpose of the general lis pendens provision in Brussels I, art. 29?
	- to avoid having parallel court proceeding. 
	

5. What is the purpose and content of the reverse lis pendens provision in Brussels I, art. 31.2?
	- protext exclusive jurisdiction clauses (art. 25) from being undermined by tactical lawsuits. 
	- the parties cant be italian torpedoed, and the agreed upon choice of court can proceed with the problem even if it was not the first court to be seised by the party.
