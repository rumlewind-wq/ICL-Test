### Judicial assistance for arbitration

- Generally, courts do not interfere with arbitral proceedings, see e.g. Model Law, art. 5

Article 5. Extent of court intervention

 In matters governed by this Law, no court shall intervene except where so provided in this Law.

- Tribunals have no coercive power, courts do have such power, so some interplay/cooperation between tribunals and courts is necessary

### Judicial assistance for arbitration

- Enforcement of arbitration agreements

- NYC art. II(3) and Model Law, art. 8

A court before which an action is brought in a matter which is the subject of an arbitration agreement shall, if a party so requests not later than when submitting his first state-ment on the substance of the dispute, refer the parties to arbitration unless it finds that the agreement is null and void, inoperative or incapable of being performed.

### Judicial assistance for arbitration

- Competence of the Arbitral Tribunal

- The competence-competence doctrine is a cornerstone in most countries and the Model Law, art. 16

- France, art. 1448 code civil procedure, the arbitration agreement is only set aside by a court if manifestly void

- China, tribunals are generally not allowed to determine their own jurisdiction

### Judicial assistance for arbitration

- Interim measures, generally accepted that parties may ask a court for interim measures even if arbitration is agreed, see e.g. Model Law, art. 9

- Interim measures, Model Law, art. 17(2)
	- Maintain or restore status quo
	- Preserving assets for enforcement
	- Preserve evidence

### Judicial assistance for arbitration

- Interim measures from **courts**, e.g. Model law, art. 9

- Interim measures from the **Arbitral Tribunal**, e.g. Model Law, ch. 4A, 2006

- Concurrent jurisdiction, UK and many others

- Italy, Argentina and DK, only courts can order interim measures

### Judicial assistance for arbitration

- Enforcement of court ordered interim measures

- Enforcement of interim measures from the Arbitral Tribunal under the Model law, art.17 H

- Exceptions listed in Model Law, art. 17 I
	- Ordinary grounds for refusal under Model Law
	- No security provided
	- The interim measure has been suspended or terminated by the Tribunal or a court
	- Not technically possible to enforce it, unless adapted

### Judicial assistance for arbitration

- Obtaining evidence through courts

- The Model law, art. 27

Court assistance in taking evidence The arbitral tribunal or a party with the approval of the arbitral tribunal may request from a competent court of this State assistance in taking evidence. The court may execute the request within its competence and according to its rules on taking evidence.

- Methods of selection
	- The lex arbitri governs
	- The parties' agreement
	- Institutional rules

- Three arbitrators

	- ICC, each party appoint one arbitrator and unless otherwise agreed, ICC selects the chair

### The Tribunal

- Failure to appoint one arbitrator or the chair
	- Model Law art. 11, a court appoints the arbitrator
	- The Institution appoints the arbitrator

- All arbitrators, also party-appointed arbitrators must be
	- Impartial and independent
		- Can consult with the appointing party about the chair
		- Cannot act as an advocate for the appointing party

### The Tribunal

- The chair
	- Appointed by the co-arbitrators, the institution or a court
	- Sometimes appointed by the counsels
	- Failure, the Institution, or the seat courts, Model Law, art. 11

- The Perfect Chair
	- Impartial
	- Bright, knowledgeable
	- Common sense and authority, but not too much authority
	- Listens carefully, thoughtful and able to decide
	- Available, not self-conscious, not arrogant
	- Good at steering proceedings and drafting awards

### The Tribunal

- Sole arbitrator
	- Should be chosen after the dispute has arisen
	- Sometimes difficult to agree at that stage
	- Agree on qualifications, exchange a list of 3-5 candidates
	- The institution, or the seat courts will choose the arbitrator, Model law art. 11

- Ad hoc arbitration
	- No institution to help with appointments
	- Appointing authority should be agreed in the arbitration clause
	- UNCITRAL ad hoc Rules, art. 6.2, the SG of the Permanent Court of Arbitration at the Hague of Arbitration at the Hague

### The Tribunal

- Obligations of arbitrators

- Impartial and independent, Model Law art. 12
	- Impartial, no bias
	- Independent, no financial interest in the outcome of the case

### The Tribunal

- The IBA Guidelines on Conflict of Interests 2014

- Apply to all arbitrators, party appointed and the chair

- Part I, General Standard

- Part II, Practical application
	- Traffic light, red, orange and green lists

### The Tribunal

- General Standard 1, every arbitrator must be and remain impartial and independent

- General standard 2.1, step down if the arbitrators has doubts

- General Standard 2.2, step down if a reasonable person would have doubts

- General standard 3, the arbitrator must disclose any circumstances which in the eyes of the parties may create doubts as to the arbi-trator's independence or impartiality

### The award

- The award
	- Types of awards
	- Validity of the award
	- Remedies and costs
	- Res judiciata
	- Confidentiality of the award
	- Post-award proceedings

### The award

- An award/ a final award
	- Resolves the dispute on the substance between the parties
	- Terminates the duties of the tribunal
	- The losing party may try to challenge it
	- The losing party may try prevent enforcement

### The award

 An order
 
- Deals with procedural issues
	- Discovery, evidence issues, places and times of hearings etc

- Usually not reviewable by a court

- If the order is sufficiently final, e.g. an order on prehearing security, it may be reviewable

- Orders can and should be objected to before the tribunal

### The award

- Partial and interim awards
	- Uncitral Rules, art. 34,1, the tribunal may make separate awards on different issues at different times
	- Substantive claims, e.g. the interpretation of a given contractual clause
	- Procedural issues, e.g. competence, choice of law for the container contract
	- Interim measures, Uncitral Rules, art. 26
	- Interim awards are not enforceable under the NYC

### The award

- Consent award
	- Sometimes parties settle the dispute before the tribunal
	- Model Law, art. 30, the parties may ask the tribunal to render an award on the basis of the settlement
	- Consent awards are enforceable under the NYC

### The award

- Default award
	- The respondent does not participate in the arbitral proceedings
	- In litigation, courts often automatically deliver a default judgment in favour of the claimant
	- In arbitration, this is not possible, thus the tribunal must still review all the evidence and the arguments, if any, by the respondent
	- Default awards are enforceable under the NYC

### The award

Formal requirements to the award, Uncitral Rules, art. 34, Model Law art. 31
- In writing
- Final and binding
- Supported by reaons
- Signed by the arbitrators
- Dated
- Place of arbitration named
- Communicated to both parties

### The award

- Remedies

- Monetary damages

- Interests
	- Limits in the lex arbitra?
	- Limits in the law of enforcement?
	- Prohibitions, eg Sharia legislation

### The award

- Declaratory award
	- Stating the rights of the parties

- Injunctive relief
	- Respondent should abstain from certain actions

- Punitive damages
	- Rarely awarded, civil law vs common law
	- Public policy
	- Hague 2005 and 2019 Judgments Conventions

### The award

Costs
- Institutional costs; administrative fees
- Arbitrators' fees
- Opponent's legal costs

Allocation of costs
- The parties' agreement
- Lex arbitra
- The tribunal decides and allocates the costs

### The award

- Res judiciata of the award

- The same issue that has been decided by the tribunal cannot be re-litigated or re-arbitrated between the parties, further proceedings are barred

- Resdicata when the award is made,communicated to the parties or when it is no longer subject to invalidity proceedings

- The exact time depends on the lex arbitra
