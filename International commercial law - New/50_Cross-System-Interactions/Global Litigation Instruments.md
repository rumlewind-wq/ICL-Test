# [[Global Litigation Instruments – Hague 2005 & 2019 Conventions]]

## Overview (source: [[ICL emne fra bøger]])
- [[Hague Choice of Court Convention 2005]]: exclusive choice-of-court agreements, indirect jurisdiction filters, recognition and enforcement regime, and the package approach linking to the 2019 Judgments Convention
- [[Hague Judgments Convention 2019]]: scope/exclusions, acceptable jurisdictional bases (Art. 5), exclusive jurisdiction rules (Art. 6), refusal grounds (Art. 7), treatment of preliminary questions, severability, and settlements; Contracting States may issue declarations to tailor coverage

## Detailed references (source: [[Bestemmelseoversigt ICL]])
- **Hague 2005**
    - Art. 1 scope; Art. 2 exclusions
    - Art. 3 formal requirements and definition of exclusive clauses
    - Art. 5 jurisdiction of the chosen court and Art. 6 obligations of non-chosen courts
    - Arts. 10–11 address public policy and punitive damages in the recognition stage
- **Hague 2019**
    - Arts. 1–2 scope/exclusions; Art. 5 jurisdictional filters; Art. 7 refusal grounds; Art. 10 punitive damages
    - Mirrors the New York Convention logic for court judgments and aims at global circulation beyond the EU

## Global cooperation pointers (source: [[Global Co-operation]])
- Hague 2005 and Hague 2019 sit alongside other global instruments such as the [[The 1955 Hague Choice of Law Convention for International Sales of Goods]] and the [[Den internationale købelov (UNCITRAL 1980 Sales of Goods Convention) (CISG)]]
