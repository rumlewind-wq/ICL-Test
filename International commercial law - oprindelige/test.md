
# **1. EU Private International Law Instruments (Jurisdiction & Judgments)**

###### **[[Brussels I Recast Regulation (EU) 1215/2012]] – Current EU system**
**Use for:**
- EU–EU civil/commercial disputes
- Jurisdiction, lis pendens, choice of court, enforcement
- Abolition of exequatur
- Priority for chosen court (art. 31(2))
- Core provisions constantly referenced:
		- art. 1(2) – scope
		- art. 4, 63 – domicile/seat of companies
		- art. 7.1 – contract jurisdiction
		- art. 7.2 – tort jurisdiction
		- art. 17–19 – consumer jurisdiction
		- art. 25 – choice of court agreements
		- art. 29 – lis pendens
		- art. 31(2) – reverse lis pendens (the “Gasser-fix”)
		- art. 32 – seising
		- art. 36, 39 – automatic enforcement
		- art. 45 – grounds for refusal
		- art. 52 – no review on substance

**This is the primary EU instrument in all exam scenarios unless an EFTA connection exists.**

---

#### **[[Brussels I Regulation 44/2001]] – Historical (Replaced by Recast)**

**Use for:**
- Understanding legacy case law (Gasser, Owusu, Car Trim)
- Comparing with Lugano 2007 (identical structure)

Do **not** apply substantively unless expressly required.

---

#### **[[Brussels Convention 1968]] – Historical**

**Use for:**
- Old case law still cited for interpretation
- Foundation of Brussels/Lugano system

Not applied today.

---

#### **[[Lugano Convention 2007]] – Current for EFTA (CH, NO, IS)**

**Use for:**
- **EU–EFTA** cross-border disputes
- Same structure as Brussels I (44/2001)
- **No Recast reforms** (e.g., no reverse lis pendens, no abolition of exequatur)

---

#### **[[Lugano Convention 1988]] – Historical (Replaced by 2007)**

**Use for:**
- Historical interpretation only
- Mirrors Brussels Convention 1968
    

---

# **2. EU Choice-of-Law Instruments (Applicable Law)**

#### **[[Rome I Regulation 593/2008]] – Current for Contracts**

**Use for:**
- Any contractual applicable law question (EU-wide)
- Choice of law, default rules, consumer, employment, insurance, overriding mandatory rules

**This is the primary choice-of-law regime in ICL.**

Governs contractual choice of law.  
Referenced provisions include:

- art. 2 – universal application
- art. 3 – party autonomy
- art. 4 – default choice of law rules (list, characteristic performance, escape clause)
- art. 6 – consumer contracts
- art. 7 – insurance contracts
- art. 8 – employment contracts
- art. 9 – overriding mandatory provisions  
---

#### **[[Rome II Regulation 864/2007]] – Current for Non-contractual Obligations**

**Use for:**
- Torts (including internet torts), product liability, IP infringement, competition damages
- Unjust enrichment, negotiorum gestio, culpa in contrahendo

Non-contractual obligations (referenced in relation to overlap with Rome I).  

---

#### **[[Rome Convention 1980]] – Historical (Replaced by Rome I)**

**Use for:**
- Contracts **before 17 December 2009**
- Historical development + interpretation (Giuliano–Lagarde Report)

---

# **3. Hague Conference Instruments (Global Litigation)**

#### **[[Hague Choice of Court Convention 2005]]**

**Use for:**
- **Exclusive** choice-of-court agreements
- Global alternative to Brussels Recast for non-EU states
- Recognition & enforcement of resulting judgments


- art. 1 – scope
- art. 2 – exclusions
- art. 3 – formal requirements + definition of exclusive clause
- art. 5 – jurisdiction of chosen court
- art. 6 – obligations of non-chosen court
- art. 10 / 11 – public policy & punitive damages  
    
---

#### **[[Hague Judgments Convention 2019]]**

**Use for:**
- Global recognition and enforcement of **non-choice-of-court** judgments
- Mirrors New York Convention logic but for court judgments
- Growing relevance in global litigation questions

- art. 1–2 – scope & exclusions
- art. 5 – jurisdictional filters
- art. 7 – refusal grounds
- art. 10 – punitive damages  
    
---

#### **[[Hague Convention 1955 on Sales]] – Historical**

**Use for:**
- Historical comparison only (seller’s-law presumption)
- Fully replaced by Rome I for EU analysis

---

# **4. International Arbitration Framework**

#### **[[UNCITRAL Model Law on International Commercial Arbitration]]**

**Use for:**
- Structure of arbitration procedure
- Competence-competence, separability
- Interim measures, annulment, recognition
- Applied when seat uses Model Law (e.g., Singapore, Denmark, Germany)

This is the **core procedural reference** for arbitration questions.

Key referenced provisions:

- art. 16 – competence-competence
- art. 30 – consent award
- art. 31 – form of award
- art. 34 – setting aside (invalidity)
- art. 36 – refusal of enforcement  

---

#### **[[UNCITRAL Arbitration Rules]]**

**Use for:**
- **Ad hoc arbitration**
- Default procedural rules when chosen by parties
- Interim measures, award form, partial awards


– art. 34 – award form requirements  


---

#### **[[New York Convention 1958]]**

**Use for:**
- Enforcement of arbitral awards **worldwide**
- Exhaustive refusal grounds (public policy, due process, invalid agreement)

This is **always** applied in arbitration enforcement questions.

Referenced articles:

- art. V(1)(a–e) – party-invoked refusal grounds
- art. V(2)(a–b) – arbitrability & public policy (ex officio)
- reciprocity rules  
    
---

#### **[[UNCITRAL Model Law on Electronic Commerce]]**

**Use for:**
- Explaining validity of **electronic arbitration agreements** or **electronic choice-of-court agreements**
- Functional equivalence doctrine

Referenced only indirectly.

# **5. International Substantive Law**

#### **[[CISG 1980 – Sale of Goods]]**

**Use for:**
- International sale of goods when applicable
- Interacts with Rome I (party autonomy)
- Nordic art. 94 reservation context

Not a PIL instrument, but often triggered in exam questions when determining applicable substantive law.

- art. 1(1)(a)
- art. 1(1)(b)
- art. 6
- art. 94 (Nordic reservation)

# **Ultra-Short Exam Navigation Guide**

When unsure which law applies, use this hierarchy:

## **1. What type of dispute?**

- **Court litigation?** → Brussels Recast / Lugano 2007
- **Arbitration?** → Model Law + NYC
- **Judgment recognition outside EU?** → Hague 2005 or Hague 2019

## **2. What legal issue?**

- **Jurisdiction?** → Brussels Recast / Lugano / Hague 2005
- **Applicable law?** → Rome I (contract) / Rome II (torts)
- **Substantive sales law?** → CISG

## **3. Where are the parties?**

- **EU–EU?** → Brussels Recast
- **EU–EFTA?** → Lugano 2007
- **EU–non-EU?** → Hague conventions or national PIL
